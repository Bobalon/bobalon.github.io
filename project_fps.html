I spent my last term as an Intern at Red Marmoset Studios Limited. I worked on their upcoming FPS (title pending), primarily designing the menus and HUD. It's in an early stage of development.

When not building directly in Unity, I worked primarily in Figma.

Beyond clarity and ease of navigation, the goal was to evoke Art Deco and prestige gangster cinema. The tilted square "diamond" shape became a recurring visual motif in the design.

The HUD is a mixture of Overwatch and Dishonored, with a different visual flair. White silhouettes clarify which weapon is currently equipped. The timer at the top appears when time is starting to run out.

Main menu sketches. Figma on the left, early concept sketches on the right.

The suit is an edited photo from google image search. The placeholder character is a quick sketch I made of Clint Eastwood, intended to be 3D player characters in the final version.

The Pause Menu is designed to look like a filing system. Some of the "files" have subcategories of their own.

The Options menu is divided into Audio (volume), Graphics (resolution/quality), Controls (button mapping etc.), and Interface (HUD/Accessibility). I've experimented with time appropriate icons, for clarity.

Older iterations to the left, newer version (set to the Bestiary) to the right.

Figma mockups for a save menu and a crafting menu for mutagens. The rectangle on the left side of the save slots is for an image representing the chapter or location of the save. The mutagen system is, at time of writing, not set in stone, so there are a few variations on that menu. The basic idea is that you dilute the mutagen with water from the sink. The instructions are currently for a Playstation controller.
