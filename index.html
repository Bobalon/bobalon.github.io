‚ÄúTrollmother‚Äù is a branching narrative game about a troll who raises a human boy. You do this by setting schedules, stat building, and conversation.

Made for an itch.io Game Boy competition with the theme "You are the monster".
The game is subject to the limitations of the Game Boy hardware, including strict limitations on the number of 8x8 tiles present in a scene and a maximum of three colours per sprite.

The full game is available on the
Trollmother Itch.io page



I can't wait for more stuff like this to come out.

 —"New Game, Old Flame" podcast

The project started simple. I knew I wanted cultural specificity, to make it unapologetically Scandinavian. I remembered a Figma menu competition for Halloween the previous year and adapted the Troll-themed menu I'd made for it.



Then I started brainstorming and sketching.
  Being a game about stat building and menu navigation, UI was central to the project from the beginning. I aimed to contain all stats and navigation within a single screen, to avoid having to go back and fourth.

All stats come in opposing pairs, to make it easier to parse and to reduce the number of stat bars.

I also started planning story trajectories to keep the writing efficient. I think games like this require a variety of end states to feel satisfying, so I started at the end, planning out potential endings before I'd written anything else.



To meet the deadline, I had to reign in the scope aggressively. No scrolling backdrops, basic sprites, very little animation, limited soundtrack, reuse assets as much as possible.

I cut anything that would take too long to fix or implement, did as much playtesting as I could manage. I consistently prioritized polish over content. You can always release updates but never undo a bad first impression.

I also added a player survey to the release, to inform future updates.




The game was featured at the 12th iteration of the Heart Projector Pop-up Arcade in Vancouver, Canada.
