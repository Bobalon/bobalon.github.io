I spent my last term as an Intern at Red Marmoset Studios Limited. I worked on their upcoming FPS (title pending), primarily designing the menus and HUD. It's in an early stage of development.

When not building directly in Unity, I worked primarily in Figma.

Beyond clarity and ease of navigation, the goal was to evoke Art Deco and prestige gangster cinema. The tilted square shape became a recurring visual motif in the design.

Below, example menu animation from the main menu. Logo removed. I chose the fonts and made the buttons, the animation, and the overall design, but the skyline and the red-eyed men are placeholders from older concept art.




The HUD is a mixture of Overwatch and Dishonored, with a different visual flair. White silhouettes clarify which weapon is currently equipped. The timer at the top appears when time is starting to run out.

Unity screenshot below.



Main menu sketches from Figma. The suit is an edited photo from google image search. The placeholder character is a quick sketch I made of Clint Eastwood, intended to be 3D player characters in the final version.



The Pause Menu is designed to look like a filing system. Some files have subcategories of their own.

The Options menu is divided into Audio (volume), Graphics (resolution/quality), Controls (button mapping etc.), and Interface (HUD/Accessibility). I've experimented with time appropriate icons, for clarity.

Older Figma iterations to the left, early Unity version to the right.



Figma mockups for a crafting menu for mutagens. The mutagen system is, at time of writing, not set in stone, so there are a few variations on that menu. The basic idea is that you dilute the mutagen with water from the sink. The instructions are currently for a Playstation controller.

‚ÄúAlltarach‚Äù is a branching illustrated novel set in a semi-fantastical version of 6th century Ireland - a generation after the passing of the Apostle Patrick. A personal project where I teamed up with a writer. I developed the visual style and created all of the art assets, while the writer wrote the text. We collaborated on things that weren‚Äôt strictly in those categories. Among other things, you can hover over underlined text to get words or names explained.



For the character illustrations, I opted for simple lineart and a limited palette. It's meant to evoke a storybook vibe, as well as the wind, rocks, and water of the Irish west coast.



I based the letters of the logo on the earliest forms of insular script I could find, with the same word in Ogham underneath. More challenging was figuring out what to put inside the initial letter. The original placeholder is a rough sketch of an abstract p√∫ca drawing I‚Äôd seen on a book cover once.

I also designed icons for Inventory and Settings. I wanted something that fit the style and time period of the game, while still clearly communicating what they are.

The bag was simple enough, "Settings" was more complicated. The universal icon for settings is a cog - but cogs are too modern. I thought of water wheels - similar, but older. The text lights up in red when you hover, consistent with the main menu.



The back and home/menu buttons are in a similar style, using the main character's house as the basis for the "home" icon.
