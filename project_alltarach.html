ALLTARACH
  • Genre: Illustrated Branching Narrative Twine Game
  • Team size: 2

MY ROLE

  • Responsible for Visual Presentation
  • Responsible for Art, including UI
  • Creating Concept Art and Mockups
  • Collaborating on UX

“Alltarach” is a branching illustrated novel set in a semi-fantastical version of 6th century Ireland - a generation after the passing of the Apostle Patrick. You follow the perspective of Bríd, a teenage orphan from a rocky island off the west coast. Her brother didn’t wake her this morning.

A personal project where I teamed up with a writer. I developed the visual style and created all of the art assets, while the writer wrote the text and the code. We collaborated on things that weren’t strictly in those categories. Among other things, you can hover over underlined text to get words or names explained.

For the character illustrations, I opted for rough lineart and a limited palette. It's meant to evoke a kind of storybook vibe, as well as the wind, rocks, and water of the Irish west coast.

I based the letters of the logo on the earliest forms of insular script I could find, with the same word in Ogham underneath. More challenging was figuring out what to put inside the initial letter. The original placeholder is a rough sketch of an abstract púca drawing I’d seen on a book cover once.

The final version is a creature I imagine to be somewhere between a badger and a horse, surrounded by dots and lines. The logo is primarily created in Illustrator, except for the initial letter sketches and the final colouring.

I also designed icons for Inventory and Settings. I wanted something that fit the style and time period of the game, while still clearly communicating what they are.

The bag was simple enough, "Settings" was more complicated. The universal icon for settings is a cog - but cogs are too modern. I thought of water wheels - similar, but older. I painted them both in greyscale, so they'd be different from the chapter illustrations without standing out too much. Handwrote descriptive text in insular script. The text lights up in red when you hover, consistent with the main menu.

The back and home/menu buttons are in a similar style, using the main character's house as the basis for the "home" icon.
