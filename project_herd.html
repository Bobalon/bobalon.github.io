• Genre: Co-op Puzzle Platformer
  • Team size: 6


“You Herd Me” is a top-down sheep herding game. Your goal is to guide as many sheep as possible to the end of the level, while activating bridges and preventing wolves from eating them. Two players cooperate and control distinct characters.

A four week long Futuregames project. My team had two Project Managers, two Programmers, and two Designers. The game was required to be 3D, to be local co-op, and to have puzzles.

I produced 13 rough poster sketches, followed by an A2 version of the winning concept.



Since the poster was finished and sent for printing in the first few days, the sheep are seen running off the edge of the map - something that was still present in the early design. This was abandoned as we started prototyping and testing, since allowing the sheep to run off the level made the game feel unfair.

I reused one of my old poster concepts for the pause menu - adding some buttons on the body of the sheep.



At the end of the level you’re awarded between 1-3 apples depending on the number of sheep you’ve successfully herded. The animation for apples popping in is 3 frames per apple - no apple, big apple, smaller final apple.
